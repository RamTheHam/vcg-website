The diagnosis

PE firms rarely replace the CEO at close.

For most senior executives who want to drive their next major deal, bringing it themselves is the most reliable route. The standard playbook is driven by the PE firm and the auction process: identify the target, run the bid, close the transaction, keep the incumbent management — or bring in a known operating partner.

Replacing the CEO upfront is risky and rare; in most cases, the existing chief executive stays in place.

For a senior executive who wants to drive a PE-backed platform — as CEO, as active chairman, as operating partner, or in any role that places them at the centre of the value creation plan — the standard process is a closed door. Waiting for the call rarely produces the seat. The route opens from the other side: by walking in with the company, the thesis, and the value creation plan already shaped.

Drive the process from the executive from the beginning.

VCG originates from that side. The executive brings the sector intelligence and the operating judgment. VCG brings the PE access, the methodology, and the capital. Together we build the opportunity and bring it to the PE firm whose strategy fits it best. The executive enters the deal as its author, in the role that fits the situation — not as someone responding to a process built around another plan.

The process

Three stages. Weeks, not months.

01 — Draw out

A structured conversation with the executive.

Sector view, ambition, network, what they would do differently if they owned it. 20 years of operating experience become an investable thesis — authored by the person who will drive the business.

02 — Shape and take to market

VCG shapes the thesis into a PE-grade package.

We identify the firms whose mandate and culture fit, and bring the opportunity to market. We negotiate the full structure — role, authority, compensation, equity, governance — so the executive can stay focused on the opportunity itself.

03 — Invest alongside

At close, we each reinvest half our origination fee back into the company.

That is the alignment signal — to the PE firm and to each other — that we believe in this enough to put our own capital on the same terms.
