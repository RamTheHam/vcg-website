For executives

You generated the value. You should be paid like you did.

Most senior executives have spent their careers generating value that flowed to other people. They took a salary and an option package — usually after the deal was shaped by someone else.

VCG exists for the executives who can change this.

Who this is for

Three things, all required:

Sector view Sharp enough to source the deal.
Network Key relationships to make the deal happen.
Standing The seller will see you as the preferred custodian of the business.

What originating means

Not a search firm. Not an operating-partner program. The seat at the table where the deal gets made — and the conviction to run the company for the next five to seven years.

You do not get paid to develop a view. You get paid for the view you already have.

The role

You shape the role.

The deal determines what the company needs at close. You decide which part of it you want to run.

CEO You want to run the company day-to-day.
Active chair You want to govern, hire the CEO, and stay close.
Operating partner on the board You want the influence without the executive seat.

Negotiated as part of the deal, not assigned afterwards. That is the difference between authoring the transaction and being placed into it.

For PE firms

Proprietary flow. The operator already in place. Skin in the game.

PE firms running management-heavy platforms need more than capital and a deal team. They need an experienced operating partner to drive the thesis, and a senior executive to anchor the platform in one of the roles it actually requires — CEO, active chairman, operating partner on the board.

VCG brings both. The thesis is proprietary. The executive is senior, sector-credible, and already shaped around the opportunity. VCG reinvests alongside at close.

Access · Operator · Execution

Access

Even top-quartile PE firms see less than 40% of available deal flow. VCG originates with the executive before the auction, so opportunities arrive proprietary, sector-credible, and shaped to a thesis the executive can run.

Operator

A senior operator in place from origination, in the role the deal requires — CEO, active chair, operating partner on the board. Not a name added late to satisfy diligence. No CEO transition risk after close.

Execution

VCG reinvests half its origination fee into the company at close, alongside the executive's rollover. Skin in the game on the same terms — visible to your IC.

VCG works with PE firms that pay operators properly. The economics are aligned because they are visible — to the executive, to the firm, and to each other.
